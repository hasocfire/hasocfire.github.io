Task 2: Identification of Conversational Hate-Speech in Code-Mixed Languages (ICHCL)

The ICHCL task aims to advance the field of code-mixed text classification, specifically in identifying hate speech and offensive content within conversational threads on social media.

As you may know, identifying hate speech within conversational threads poses unique challenges, as offensive language and profanity may not be evident in individual tweets, comments, or responses. However, by examining the parent content and the contextual information, it becomes possible to identify such content accurately.

To address the scarcity of labelled data and reduce the cost associated with annotating large datasets, we are introducing a novel approach this year. Participants will be provided with an unlabeled training dataset and a labelled test dataset consisting of approximately 1000 code-mixed Hindi samples.

The shared task will be divided into two subtasks:

Task 2a: Binary Classification of Conversational Tweets with Tree-Structured Data

- (NOT) Non-Hate-Offensive: Tweets, comments, or replies that do not contain hate speech, offensive language, or profanity.

- (HOF) Hate and Offensive: Tweets, comments, or replies that contain hate speech, offensive language, or profanity in themselves or support hate expressed in the parent tweet.

Task 2b: Classification of Conversational Tweets with Tree-Structured Data into Specific Forms of Hate

- (SHOF) Standalone Hate: Tweets, comments, or replies that contain hate speech, offensive language, or profanity in themselves.

- (CHOF) Contextual Hate: Comments or replies supporting hate speech, offence, and profanity expressed in the parent. This includes affirming the hate with positive sentiment and displaying apparent hate.

- (NONE) Non-Hate: Tweets, comments, or replies that do not contain hate speech, offensive language, or profanity in themselves.

You can find more detailed information about subtasks 2a and 2b [ICHCL Webpage].

A crucial requirement is that participants must leverage the unlabeled data to predict outcomes on the test dataset. Additionally, participants will be asked to submit a link to their GitHub repository to ensure compliance with this constraint.

The evaluation metric for Task 1 and 2 will be Macro F1 score.
